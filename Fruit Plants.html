Fruit Plants

INTRODUCTION

Fruit plants are those that produce edible fruits as a result of the reproductive process. They vary widely in terms of size, growth habits, and the types of fruits they yield. Tree fruits are produced by perennial plants that grow into large, woody structures. They can live for many years, often bearing fruit annually once mature. The trees can vary in height, from small dwarf varieties to large, towering specimens. Shrub fruits are produced by smaller, bush-like plants. These plants usually have multiple stems and can be deciduous or evergreen. Shrubs are often easier to manage and harvest compared to large trees. Vine fruits grow on long, trailing plants that require support to climb. These plants can be annual or perennial and are often grown on trellises, fences, or other structures to maximize space and sun exposure. Fruit plants produce fruits as part of their reproductive cycle. The fruit typically contains seeds, which help propagate the plant. Different fruit plants are adapted to specific climates. For example, temperate fruits like apples thrive in cooler climates, while tropical fruits like mangoes prefer warm, humid conditions. Fruit plants have varying soil and water requirements, with some preferring well-drained soils and others thriving in more water-retentive environments. The time and method of harvesting vary by plant, with some fruits being hand-picked and others requiring special tools or techniques. Fruit plants play a vital role in agriculture, providing nutritious food, economic benefits, and contributing to biodiversity in various ecosystems.
